LET'S PLAY
Mobile App — Client Requirements Questionnaire
MVP Scope Clarification | Version 1.0
Purpose of This Document
This questionnaire has been prepared following a detailed review of the Let's Play product
scope document. It captures areas where the current specification contains conflicts, gaps,
or open decisions that must be resolved before detailed development can begin.
Each question is grouped by theme and includes a brief rationale explaining why the answer
is needed. We recommend working through this document with your product lead, legal
counsel (where applicable), and any relevant stakeholders before the next planning session.
Section 1 — Direct Scope Conflicts
The following questions arise from features confirmed in sections 1–7 of the product scope
that directly conflict with or contradict the MVP definition in section 8.
1A | Game Departure & Cancellation
# Question Why this matters
1 What should happen when a player leaves a free game
they have already joined? Should their spot become
available immediately, or should the host be notified
first?
"Leave game" is in the MVP but
the behaviour on departure is
not defined in the spec.
2 The scope states that game creators cannot cancel or
delete a game. Is this still the intended behaviour at
MVP? If so, how does a creator close a game that is no
longer happening?
Without a cancellation path,
joined players have no way of
knowing a game has been
abandoned.
3 Should joined players receive a notification or alert
when a game they signed up for has no active host
activity for a defined period of time?
Relates to trust and participant
experience when a creator
goes inactive.
1B | Game Messages & Moderation
# Question Why this matters
1 Game messages are included in the MVP. Who is
responsible for moderating content posted in a game
message thread — the game creator, a designated
moderator, or the platform team?
Section 11.1 confirms
moderation rules are still
undefined. Launching
messages without moderation
creates a safety risk.
2 Can a game creator remove or hide an individual
message posted by a participant in their game?
Moderator permission scope is
listed as an open question in
§11.2.
3 Should users be able to report a specific game
message? If so, what action should the platform take
and within what timeframe?
User reporting flows are
required per §11.2 but have not
been designed.
4 Are there any prohibited content categories that must
be enforced at MVP (e.g. hate speech, spam, personal
contact details)? If so, is this enforced manually,
automatically, or both?
Required to define community
standards before launching a
messaging feature.
1C | Coach & Pro Roles at MVP
The MVP defers all verification (player, coach, pro) to a post-MVP phase, but sections 4.2
and 4.3 define coaches and pros as roles that require verification to exist. The following
questions resolve this conflict.
# Question Why this matters
1 Should coach and pro roles be available at all during
the MVP phase, given that the verification process
required to grant those roles is not yet built?
If coaches and pros can self-
declare at MVP without
verification, it undermines the
trust model defined in the
product spec.
2 If coach/pro roles are deferred to post-MVP, how will
the platform handle users who apply for those roles
during the MVP period — will applications be queued,
rejected, or held?
Affects both user
communication and backend
data handling during the MVP
period.
3 If a lighter review process is acceptable for MVP coach
verification, what is the minimum acceptable evidence
required to grant coach status?
Section 4.2 notes coach
verification may require a lighter
review than pro verification.
Section 2 — Missing Scope Decisions
The following questions relate to features that are confirmed or described in sections 1–7 of
the product scope but have not been assigned to the MVP or post-MVP phase in section 8.

in-app sharing as well as external platforms including Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, email,
and text message. This feature does not appear in the MVP or post-MVP list in section 8.

should it be formally moved to the post-MVP backlog?
The feature is confirmed in
§11.1 but has no scope
placement — it needs a
decision before build planning
begins.
2 For MVP, is in-app sharing (sharing a game to another
Let's Play user) sufficient, or is external sharing via the
The two sharing modes have
different implementation

scope avoids over-building.
3 Should game share links be publicly accessible (i.e.
viewable without a Let's Play account), or only
accessible to existing app users?
Determines whether the
platform needs public-facing

2B | Game Moderation & Moderator Roles
Section 11.1 confirms that game creators can promote a joined player to moderator status,
and that moderators can edit game details. This feature is not mentioned in section 8's MVP
or post-MVP list.
# Question Why this matters
1 Is the moderator promotion feature intended for the
MVP phase, post-MVP, or is it being reconsidered?
The feature is confirmed in
§11.1 but has no roadmap
placement.
2 What specific permissions should a moderator have?
For example: can they edit game details, remove
participants, delete messages, or cancel the game?
Listed as an open question in
§11.2. Must be defined before
the feature can be designed or
built.
3 Can a game creator demote or remove a moderator
they have promoted? If so, what happens to any
changes the moderator made while in that role?
Role reversibility affects data
integrity and needs a defined
behaviour.
2C | Notifications
Section 10 defines a full set of notification events for the platform. Section 8 excludes
"advanced notification settings" but does not clarify whether basic notifications are in scope
for the MVP. The app's screen map (§7.6) includes notification details as part of the Inbox
tab, implying some notification support is expected at launch.
# Question Why this matters
1 Which notification events are required at MVP? At a
minimum, should users receive push notifications when:

updated, or (c) they receive a game message?
The scope does not separate
basic from advanced
notifications. Without clarity, the
engineering team cannot plan
notification infrastructure.
2 Should notification preferences (e.g. muting a specific
game) be configurable by the user at MVP, or will all
notifications be on by default with no user controls until
Affects settings UI scope and
backend notification storage
requirements.
post-MVP?
3 Should notifications be delivered via push only, or
should in-app notification badges and an inbox
notification list also be supported at MVP?
Determines whether a
dedicated notifications screen
or badge system needs to be
built for launch.
2D | Game Creation Fields
Section 11.1 confirms that age group and gender group are required fields during game
creation. Section 8 lists game creation as an MVP feature but does not call out these fields
explicitly, creating a risk they could be overlooked during implementation scoping.
# Question Why this matters
1 Should age group (Young Adult, Adult, Senior, All) and
gender group (Male, Female, All) fields be mandatory or
optional when creating a game at MVP?
Mandatory fields affect form
validation logic and onboarding
flow design.
2 Should the age group and gender group filters appear
in the game discovery search at MVP, or only in the
game creation form?
If these fields are in creation
but not discovery, their value is
limited at launch.
3 Are there any inclusivity, legal, or regional
considerations that should influence how age and
gender group options are displayed or labelled in the
app?
Particularly relevant for markets
with specific data or anti-
discrimination regulations.
Section 3 — Supporting Product Decisions
The following questions address items from sections 9–13 that have dependencies on the
MVP scope and should be resolved alongside the questions above.
3A | Admin & Backend Readiness
# Question Why this matters
1 Section 9 states the backend should be designed with
admin workflows in mind even if the admin dashboard
is post-MVP. Are there specific admin capabilities (e.g.
manually banning a user or removing a game) that
must be operable at MVP, even if only via internal tools
or direct database access?
Without any admin capability at
launch, the team has no way to
respond to safety or moderation
incidents.
2 Who on the client side is responsible for handling
verification applications and moderation incidents
during the MVP period, before an admin dashboard is
built?
Defines internal operational
requirements and support
staffing for launch.
3B | Payments & Refunds
Paid lessons and paid pro-status games are out of scope for MVP. However, section 11.2
lists refund and cancellation policy as an open question, and payment infrastructure
decisions made at MVP may affect post-MVP payment integration.
# Question Why this matters
1 Has a payment provider been selected for the platform
(e.g. Stripe, RevenueCat, Apple/Google in-app
purchase)? If not, when does this decision need to be
made relative to the MVP launch?
Payment provider selection
affects backend architecture
and may require early
integration work even for MVP.
2 What is the platform's intended refund policy for paid
lessons and paid pro-status games? For example, is a
full refund available up to 24 hours before the activity?
Required for terms of service
and payment provider
configuration. Listed as an
open question in §11.2.
3 What percentage or flat fee does the platform take from
paid lessons and paid pro-status games?
Listed as open in §11.2.
Needed before any payment UI
or payout logic can be scoped.
3C | Ratings
# Question Why this matters
1 What criteria should ratings be based on? For example:
punctuality, sportsmanship, communication, skill
representation. Should ratings use a numeric scale,
stars, or a tag-based system?
Listed as an open question in
§11.2. Must be defined before
any ratings UI or logic can be
designed.
2 Should ratings be publicly visible on a player's profile,
visible only to the rated user, or partially visible (e.g.
aggregate score public, individual reviews private)?
Listed as an open question in
§11.2. Has direct impact on
profile design and privacy
policy.
3 Should coaches and pros be verified per sport or game
(e.g. verified basketball coach, unverified tennis coach),
or should verification apply globally to a user's account
across all sports?
Listed as an open question in
§11.2. Affects the data model
for verification and how it is
displayed on profiles.
