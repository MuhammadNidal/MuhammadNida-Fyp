Let’s Play — Client Questionnaire Responses
Section 1 — Direct Scope Conflicts
1A — Game Departure & Cancellation
1A.1 What should happen when a player leaves a free game they have
already joined? Should their spot become available immediately, or
should the host be notified first?
Answer:
For a standard free game, when a non-organizer player leaves, their spot should
become available immediately.
The organizer should receive a notification, but the organizer does not need to
approve the departure.
MVP behavior:
 Player taps Leave Game.
 Player is removed from the participant list.
 The available spot count increases immediately.
 The current organizer receives a notification.
 The game remains active and discoverable if it still has open spots and
meets discovery criteria.
Important exception:
If the player leaving is the current organizer and other players have joined, they
cannot fully leave until organizer responsibility is transferred to another joined
player.
1A.2 The scope states that game creators cannot cancel or delete a
game. Is this still the intended behavior at MVP? If so, how does a
creator close a game that is no longer happening?
Answer:
For standard player-created games, the intended behavior is that the game
cannot be canceled or deleted once other players have joined.
A standard game should not collapse just because the original creator can no
longer attend. If players have already made plans to meet and play, the game
should continue for the group.
However, to prevent orphaned games, every active standard game must always
have one organizer.
MVP behavior for standard games:
 The original creator is the initial organizer.
 If no other players have joined, the creator/organizer can cancel, close, or
delete the game.
 Once one or more players have joined, the game cannot be deleted.
 Once one or more players have joined, the game cannot be canceled simply
because the organizer can no longer attend.
 If the organizer wants to leave, they must transfer organizer responsibility
to another joined player first.
 The new organizer receives game-management permissions.
 Joined players are notified when organizer responsibility changes.
 The game remains active for all joined participants.
 The game remains discoverable if it still has open spots and meets
discovery criteria.
Cancellation exception:
Coach lessons and pro games are host-dependent. Since the coach/pro is central
to the value of the event, those event types can be canceled by the host.
MVP behavior for coach lessons and pro games:
 A coach can cancel a lesson they created.
 A pro can cancel a pro game they created.
 Canceled lessons and pro games remain in the system as canceled records,
not deleted records.
 Joined participants are notified when a lesson or pro game is canceled.
 Canceled lessons and pro games no longer accept new participants.
 Refund/payment behavior must follow the payment and refund policy
defined for paid activities.
1A.3 Should joined players receive a notification or alert when a game
they signed up for has no active host activity for a defined period of
time?
Answer:
Not for MVP.
For standard games, the platform should not treat the original creator as a
required host. The current organizer is responsible for basic game management,
but the game itself is community-driven.
MVP behavior:
 No automated inactive-host alerts.
 Players rely on game details, organizer updates, and game messages.
 If the organizer can no longer attend, organizer responsibility must
transfer before they can leave.
Post-MVP consideration:
The platform may later add host confirmation prompts, automated reminders, or
game health indicators.
1B — Game Messages & Moderation
1B.1 Game messages are included in the MVP. Who is responsible for
moderating content posted in a game message thread — the game
creator, a designated moderator, or the platform team?
Answer:
For MVP, moderation should be a shared responsibility between the current
game organizer and the platform team.
The organizer should have lightweight controls for the game thread. The
platform team should have final authority to review reports and take
enforcement action.
MVP behavior:
 The current organizer can report problematic messages.
 The current organizer may have limited ability to hide messages in their
own game thread.
 Users can report specific messages.
 Platform admins review reports and take enforcement action.
 Separate moderator promotion is post-MVP.
Rationale:
Organizer transfer is part of MVP to avoid orphaned games. Moderator
promotion beyond organizer transfer is post-MVP.
1B.2 Can a game creator remove or hide an individual message posted
by a participant in their game?
Answer:
For MVP, the current organizer should be able to hide an individual message, not
hard delete it.
MVP behavior:
 Current organizer can hide a message from the visible game thread.
 Hidden messages remain stored for audit/review purposes.
 Hidden messages can be reviewed by platform admins.
 The original message author may see a generic state such as “This message
was removed.”
Rationale:
Hiding gives the organizer basic safety control while preserving data for review.
1B.3 Should users be able to report a specific game message? If so,
what action should the platform take and within what timeframe?
Answer:
Yes. Users should be able to report a specific game message at MVP.
MVP behavior:
 User can report an individual game message.
 Report reasons should be simple at MVP:
o Harassment or abuse
o Hate or discriminatory content
o Spam or scam
o Threats or safety concern
o Other
 Reported messages are flagged for platform review.
 Platform admins review reports manually.
Response timeframe:
Do not promise a strict public SLA at MVP unless the operations team is staffed to
support it. Internally, urgent safety reports should be prioritized.
1B.4 Are there any prohibited content categories that must be
enforced at MVP? If so, is this enforced manually, automatically, or
both?
Answer:
Yes. MVP should include basic prohibited content categories and manual
enforcement.
MVP prohibited content categories:
 Harassment or bullying
 Hate speech or discriminatory content
 Threats or encouragement of violence
 Spam, scams, or fraudulent activity
 Sexual or explicit content
 Illegal activity
 Doxxing or sharing someone else’s private personal information
 Impersonation
 Content that creates a safety risk for users
MVP enforcement:
 Manual enforcement by platform admins.
 User reporting for messages and users.
 Organizer-level message hiding for game threads.
Automatic moderation can be considered post-MVP.
1C — Coach & Pro Roles at MVP
1C.1 Should coach and pro roles be available at all during the MVP
phase, given that the verification process required to grant those roles
is not yet built?
Answer:
Yes. Coach and pro roles should be part of MVP.
Coach/pro roles, lessons, pro games, verification, payments, and subscriptions are
part of the MVP business model and should be included in the launch scope.
However, users should not be able to self-declare as coaches or pros without
review.
MVP behavior:
 All users begin as players.
 Users can apply for coach status.
 Users can apply for pro status.
 Platform admins manually review coach/pro applications.
 Approved coaches can create lessons.
 Approved pros can create lessons and pro games.
 Coach/pro status requires an active subscription unless comped by an
admin where applicable.
 Users without coach/pro privileges cannot create lessons or pro games.
1C.2 If coach/pro roles are deferred to post-MVP, how will the
platform handle users who apply for those roles during the MVP
period — will applications be queued, rejected, or held?
Answer:
Not applicable. Coach and pro roles are not deferred; they are part of MVP.
MVP behavior:
 Users can apply for coach or pro status during MVP.
 Applications are submitted for manual platform/admin review.
 Applications can be approved, rejected, or returned for more information.
 Approved users receive the appropriate role and creation privileges.
 Subscription status must be active before coach/pro privileges are
available, unless the role is comped by an admin where applicable.
1C.3 If a lighter review process is acceptable for MVP coach
verification, what is the minimum acceptable evidence required to
grant coach status?
Answer:
A lighter coach review process is acceptable for MVP, but it should still require
manual approval.
Minimum coach verification evidence for MVP:
 Sport or game the user wants to coach.
 Short coaching experience summary.
 Relevant certification, league affiliation, organization affiliation, or
coaching history, if available.
 Link to a public profile, website, social profile, league page, or other
supporting evidence, if available.
 Optional admin notes from the review process.
The platform should allow admins to approve coach status based on reasonable
evidence, even if the user does not have formal certification.
Pro verification should require stronger evidence, such as professional
playing history, team affiliation, tournament record, ranking, public profile,
press, or other proof of professional-level experience.
Section 2 — Missing Scope Decisions
2A — Game Sharing
2A.1 Is game sharing intended to be part of the MVP, or should it be
formally moved to the post-MVP backlog?
Answer:
Game sharing should be part of MVP, with limited scope.
MVP sharing scope:
 External sharing through the native mobile share sheet.
 Share content should include a game title, date/time, location summary,
and link or app-specific reference.
Post-MVP sharing scope:
 Rich in-app sharing to specific users or groups.
 Share tracking.
 Invite flows.
 Public web preview pages, if needed.
2A.2 For MVP, is in-app sharing sufficient, or is external sharing via
the native mobile share sheet also required at launch?
Answer:
External sharing via the native mobile share sheet is required at launch.
In-app sharing to other Let’s Play users can be post-MVP unless it is simple to
support using the existing user/profile system.
MVP behavior:
 User taps Share from a game detail page.
 Native share sheet opens.
 User can share through installed apps such as text, email, Instagram,
Facebook, TikTok, and other available apps.
Rationale:
External sharing is likely more valuable at launch because the network will be
small. Users need to invite people outside the platform.
2A.3 Should game share links be publicly accessible, viewable without
a Let’s Play account, or only accessible to existing app users?
Answer:
For MVP, shared links should route users into the app experience when possible
and encourage account creation/login if the recipient is not already a user.
Public web game detail pages are not required for MVP.
MVP behavior:
 Share link opens the app if installed.
 If the app is not installed, the link routes to an app download or landing
experience.
 Full game participation requires a Let’s Play account.
Post-MVP consideration:
Public web preview pages may be added later to improve conversion from
external sharing.
2B — Game Moderation & Moderator Roles
2B.1 Is the moderator promotion feature intended for the MVP phase,
post-MVP, or is it being reconsidered?
Answer:
Moderator promotion should be post-MVP.
For MVP, the game should have a single current organizer. Organizer transfer is
part of MVP, but broader moderator promotion is not.
MVP behavior:
 Every active standard game must have one organizer.
 The organizer can transfer organizer responsibility to another joined
player.
 There are no additional moderator roles beyond the current organizer.
Post-MVP behavior:
 A game may support multiple moderators.
 Moderators may be granted specific permissions by the organizer.
2B.2 What specific permissions should a moderator have? For
example: can they edit game details, remove participants, delete
messages, or cancel the game?
Answer:
For MVP, this applies to the current organizer, not a separate moderator role.
MVP organizer permissions:
 Edit standard game details.
 Hide inappropriate game messages.
 Transfer organizer responsibility to another joined player.
 Manage basic participant/game state where required.
Important limitation:
For standard games with joined participants, the organizer cannot cancel or
delete the game simply because they can no longer attend.
Post-MVP moderator permissions to evaluate:
 Edit game details.
 Hide game messages.
 Remove participants.
 Close participation when the game is full or no longer accepting players.
The ability to cancel a standard game should remain limited or unavailable once
participants have joined.
2B.3 Can a game creator demote or remove a moderator they have
promoted? If so, what happens to any changes the moderator made
while in that role?
Answer:
Moderator demotion/removal is post-MVP because moderator promotion is post-
MVP.
For MVP, organizer transfer should be supported.
MVP behavior:
 Current organizer can transfer organizer responsibility to another joined
player.
 Once transferred, the new organizer becomes the current organizer.
 The previous organizer no longer has organizer permissions unless
organizer responsibility is transferred back.
 Changes made while a user was organizer remain in place.
 The system should keep an audit record of organizer changes and
important game edits.
2C — Notifications
2C.1 Which notification events are required at MVP? At a minimum,
should users receive push notifications when: (a) someone joins their
game, (b) a game they joined is updated, or (c) they receive a game
message?
Answer:
MVP should include a limited set of basic push notifications.
Required MVP notification events:
 Someone joins a game the user organizes.
 A game the user joined is updated.
 Organizer responsibility is transferred for a game the user joined.
 A lesson or pro game the user joined is canceled.
 A user receives a new game message in a game they joined.
 Coach/pro application status changes.
 Coach/pro subscription status requires user attention.
Post-MVP notification events:
 Direct message notifications.
 Group message notifications.
 Followed user activity notifications.
 Advanced reminder notifications.
 Notification digests.
2C.2 Should notification preferences, such as muting a specific game,
be configurable by the user at MVP, or will all notifications be on by
default with no user controls until post-MVP?
Answer:
For MVP, notification preferences should be minimal.
MVP behavior:
 Notifications are on by default after the user grants OS-level notification
permission.
 Users can rely on iOS/Android system settings to disable notifications.
 In-app notification preferences, including muting a specific game, are post-
MVP.
2C.3 Should notifications be delivered via push only, or should in-app
notification badges and an inbox notification list also be supported at
MVP?
Answer:
For MVP, support push notifications and basic in-app unread indicators/badges
where necessary.
A full notification inbox/list can be post-MVP.
MVP behavior:
 Push notifications for required MVP events.
 Basic unread state or badge for game messages.
 Basic status indicators for coach/pro application or subscription state,
where needed.
 No full notification center required at launch.
Post-MVP behavior:
 Notification inbox/list.
 Notification detail screen.
 Notification preferences.
 Per-game mute controls.
2D — Game Creation Fields
2D.1 Should age group and gender group fields be mandatory or
optional when creating a game at MVP?
Answer:
Age group and gender group should be mandatory at MVP, but both should
include an All option.
MVP behavior:
 Age Group is required.
 Gender/Participation Group is required.
 Default selection may be All to reduce friction.
2D.2 Should the age group and gender group filters appear in the
game discovery search at MVP, or only in the game creation form?
Answer:
They should appear in game discovery filters at MVP.
MVP behavior:
 Users can filter games by age group.
 Users can filter games by gender/participation group.
 Games marked All should appear broadly unless filter logic intentionally
excludes them.
2D.3 Are there any inclusivity, legal, or regional considerations that
should influence how age and gender group options are displayed or
labelled in the app?
Answer:
Yes. This should be reviewed carefully before launch.
Recommended MVP approach:
 Use neutral, user-friendly labels.
 Include All as an option for both age group and gender/participation
group.
 Avoid presenting gender group as a statement about a user’s identity;
frame it as the intended participation group for a specific game.
 Avoid collecting unnecessary sensitive profile data unless required for
product functionality.
 Consider legal review before launch, especially if the platform is used
across different regions.
Potential label adjustment:
Instead of Gender Group, consider Participation Group if the product team
wants a softer label.
Section 3 — Supporting Product Decisions
3A — Admin & Backend Readiness
3A.1 Are there specific admin capabilities that must be operable at
MVP, even if only via internal tools or direct database access?
Answer:
Yes. MVP requires internal admin capabilities because coach/pro verification,
subscriptions, payments, user-generated content, and moderation are all part of
launch scope.
Required MVP admin capabilities:
 View users.
 Disable or ban a user.
 View games, lessons, and pro games.
 View coach/pro applications.
 Approve, reject, or request more information for coach/pro applications.
 Assign or remove coach/pro status.
 View coach/pro subscription status.
 Comp coach/pro/pro-related subscription access where applicable.
 Cancel or remove inappropriate activities from discovery if required for
safety/moderation.
 View reported game messages.
 Hide or remove reported game messages.
 View basic report history.
 View payment/registration status for paid lessons and paid pro games.
These capabilities can be supported through lightweight internal tooling, a
protected admin interface, or carefully controlled direct database operations if
necessary.
3A.2 Who on the client side is responsible for handling verification
applications and moderation incidents during the MVP period, before
an admin dashboard is built?
Answer:
The client/product team must identify an internal owner before launch.
MVP requirement:
 Assign at least one internal operations/admin owner for coach/pro
verification.
 Assign at least one internal operations/admin owner for moderation
reports.
 Define an escalation path for urgent safety issues.
 Define who has permission to approve/reject coach and pro applications.
 Define who has permission to ban users, hide messages, remove activities
from discovery, and comp subscriptions.
Open client decision:
Name the specific person or team responsible for MVP verification and
moderation operations.
3B — Payments & Refunds
3B.1 Has a payment provider been selected for the platform? If not,
when does this decision need to be made relative to the MVP launch?
Answer:
A payment provider must be selected for MVP because paid lessons, paid pro
games, and coach/pro subscriptions are part of MVP.
MVP requirement:
 Select payment provider before detailed MVP implementation begins.
 Confirm whether paid event registration is handled through Stripe or
another external payment provider.
 Confirm whether coach/pro subscriptions are handled through Stripe,
RevenueCat, Apple/Google in-app purchase, or another subscription
provider.
 Confirm platform rules around in-app purchases depending on whether
subscriptions unlock digital features, real-world services, or both.
Open decision:
Final payment provider is not selected yet.
Recommendation:
Evaluate Stripe for paid real-world activities and marketplace-style payments.
Evaluate RevenueCat or direct Apple/Google in-app purchase if app-store-
managed subscriptions are required.
3B.2 What is the platform’s intended refund policy for paid lessons
and paid pro-status games?
Answer:
Refund policy must be defined before MVP launch because paid lessons and paid
pro games are part of MVP.
Recommended starting policy for review:
 Full refund if the participant cancels at least 24 hours before the activity.
 No automatic refund within 24 hours unless the host cancels or there are
extenuating circumstances.
 Full refund if the coach/pro host cancels.
 Platform/admin discretion for disputes.
This policy should be reviewed with legal and operations before implementation.
Open decision:
Final refund policy is not yet approved.
3B.3 What percentage or flat fee does the platform take from paid
lessons and paid pro-status games?
Answer:
This is a required MVP business decision because paid lessons and paid pro
games are part of MVP.
Open decision:
The platform fee has not been finalized.
Potential models include:
 Percentage-based platform fee.
 Flat fee per transaction.
 Combination of percentage plus flat fee.
 Subscription-only model with no per-transaction fee.
This decision should be made before payment UI, payout logic, and financial
reporting are finalized.
